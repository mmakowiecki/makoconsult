# MAKO Consult — Design Brief

*The visual direction for the website. Written for a designer coming to this project fresh, based on the existing brand kit.*

---

## The Feel I'm After

The site should feel like a premium European consultancy — precise, warm, and confident. Think the measured authority of *The Economist*, the structured calm of a well-regarded boutique firm, the warmth of someone who is senior enough to be direct without being cold.

This is not a tech startup, not a creative agency, and not a generic corporate services provider. The design should signal that the person behind it has taste, rigour, and a point of view.

**In three words: Precise. Warm. Confident.**

White space is important. Hierarchy should be clear. Everything on the page should earn its place.

---

## Brand Kit

A brand kit is already established and available. The designer should work within it, not around it.

### Logo

The MAKO Consult logo exists in several variations, all provided as SVG files:

- **Horizontal** — the primary logo, wordmark alongside the diamond mark
- **Vertical** — stacked version for contexts where that suits better
- **Diamond only** — the isolated mark, for compact contexts (favicon, mobile header)

Each comes in four colour treatments: dark (on white), white (on dark), grey, and a yellow/solar variant. The yellow version is for the logo mark accent only and should not be replicated as a UI colour.

### Typography

Two typefaces:

**Glacial Indifference** — for headings. A geometric sans-serif with clean, open letterforms. Conveys precision without coldness. Files are available to self-host.

**Montserrat** — for body copy, navigation, buttons, and UI text. Available via Google Fonts. Used across weights from light (300) through to bold (700).

## Photography

I have a set of custom stock images that form the visual backbone of the site. They share a consistent character: warm natural light, practical work-in-progress settings, muted earthy tones. They are not staged or generic — they have an editorial, European quality that fits the brand well.

I also have a professional portrait available for the about page.

---

## Tone by Page

Each page has a slightly different emotional job:

- **Home** — decisive and grounding. The reader should feel they've found someone who understands their problem.
- **Services** — clear and structured. The reader should finish the page knowing exactly what I do and how.
- **About** — personal but not casual. The reader should feel they're learning about a real person with genuine experience and a considered way of working.
- **Contact** — low friction and welcoming. No pressure, just an easy next step.

---

## What Good Looks Like

A visitor who is a marketing director or founder at a mid-size B2B company should land on this site and immediately feel: *this person is serious, experienced, and worth talking to.* They should not feel like they're being sold to. They should feel like they've found someone who could genuinely help them — and that the conversation is an easy, natural next step.
