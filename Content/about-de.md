About MAKO Consult

# Background & Approach

I've spent fifteen years building marketing strategies across global brands and leading agencies. Five years at Imperial Brands in Bristol, working on global brand strategy for international FMCG markets. Brand and communications at Bacardi. Strategic marketing at 4flow, developing growth strategies for B2B logistics and supply chain solutions. Agency experience at Serviceplan, Scholz & Friends, and Weischer Media — from brand positioning and communication strategy to media strategy development and execution.

This path gave me dual perspective: how companies make strategic decisions internally, and how those strategies translate into execution. I've developed brand positioning and messaging frameworks, built communication and media strategies, worked across B2B and B2C contexts. Projects from Bristol to Phnom Penh to Abidjan — repositioning consumer brands in Southeast Asian markets, developing B2B growth systems in Germany, building strategies across industries and cultural contexts.

What drives my work is curiosity about how businesses actually operate. I'm passionate about understanding the specific market dynamics that shape each situation — diving deep into research, exploring unfamiliar territories, learning what makes a context unique. My scientific background taught me to structure complexity and analyze data rigorously, which shapes how I approach every project.

I challenge assumptions not to be contrarian, but because comfortable thinking rarely leads to the best solutions. I look for new paths grounded in evidence and built through understanding — strategies that fit real constraints, not theoretical ideals.

That's what MAKO Consult brings: strategic clarity built specifically for your business context, through a combination of experience, analytical rigor, and genuine curiosity about how to make it work.

Philosophy

## How I Work

Strategic clarity comes from understanding your specific context first, then building solutions that fit your reality.

Discovery Before Solutions

I don't start with solutions. I start with understanding your specific business context — then build strategy that fits your reality, not a template.

No Generic Frameworks

Every business has unique market dynamics, competitive pressures, and internal constraints. Generic frameworks miss what actually matters in your situation.

The Dual Perspective

I've seen strategy from both sides — how companies make decisions internally, and how those strategies get executed in practice. That combination is rare.

International Pattern Recognition

Working across markets gives perspective. I've seen how different companies solve similar problems in fundamentally different ways — that helps me spot solutions others miss.

Modern Tools, Pragmatic Application

I use AI and modern data methods — but as tools that serve strategy, not as the strategy itself. Technology should make things clearer, not more complex.
