# MAKO Consult — Website Brief

*What I want my website to do and why. Written for a web designer coming to this project fresh.*

---

## Who I Am and What I Do

I'm Michael Makowiecki, a strategic marketing consultant based in Hamburg, Germany. I work with European B2B companies and scale-ups that have outgrown reactive marketing but haven't yet built the strategic foundation to replace it. MAKO Consult is a one-person consultancy — I am the product. There is no team behind me.

My work sits across four areas: brand positioning, communication strategy, data and analytics, and B2B sales and marketing alignment. Engagements typically start with positioning and evolve from there.

---

## What the Website Needs to Achieve

This is a marketing and credibility tool, not a brochure. Its job is to:

1. **Establish immediate credibility** with a senior B2B audience — marketing directors, founders, and CEOs who can smell generic consultancy positioning from a mile away.
2. **Communicate what I actually do** without hiding behind jargon or vague value propositions.
3. **Create a direct path to conversation** — the primary conversion is a booked discovery call or a sent message.
4. **Reflect the quality of my work** — the site itself should be evidence that I understand positioning, clarity, and considered judgment.

The tone should feel like talking to a trusted senior colleague, not reading a sales pitch. I'm not trying to impress everyone — only the right people.

---

## The Audience

My clients are typically:

- Marketing leaders or founders at B2B companies with 20–200 employees
- Based in Germany or broader Europe, often with international operations
- Experienced enough to recognise when their marketing lacks strategic direction
- Skeptical of consultants who promise transformation without understanding context first

They are not easily impressed by flashy design or vague claims. They respond to specificity, evidence of rigour, and a sense that I understand their reality.

---

## Pages

### Home

The home page needs to earn attention quickly. My audience is busy and skeptical — if the opening doesn't resonate, they won't scroll. The core message is: marketing without strategic foundation is expensive and exhausting, and I help fix that.

From there, the page should briefly introduce the four service areas (without going deep), offer a glimpse of who I am and what drives my approach, and close with a clear call to action. A logo strip of companies I've worked with or for would build credibility here.

The primary CTA throughout the site is booking a free discovery call.

### Services

This page goes deeper on what I actually do. Each of the four service areas should feel like a coherent discipline, not a menu item — with a short description of what it involves and what it produces. The page should also explain how I typically work: a structured process from discovery through to delivery and ongoing support.

The overall tone should make clear that I'm a strategist, not an executor. I build frameworks and direction; I don't write copy or run campaigns.

### About

This is where I need to earn personal trust. My background is genuinely unusual — fifteen years across global FMCG brands (Imperial Brands, Bacardi), B2B companies (4flow), and leading agencies (Serviceplan, Scholz & Friends, Weischer). That combination of client-side and agency-side experience, across both B2B and B2C, is a real differentiator.

The about page should tell that story without it reading like a LinkedIn profile. It should also give a sense of how I think — my philosophy around starting with understanding before recommending, challenging assumptions, and using modern tools pragmatically.

A professional photo is available.

### Contact

Simple and frictionless. Two routes to reach me: a Calendly embed for booking a discovery call directly, and a contact form for those who prefer to write first. A small sidebar with basic contact information — Hamburg-based, European B2B focus, responds within 24 hours.

No pressure, no complicated forms. The goal is to remove any friction between interest and conversation.

---

## What the Site Is Not

- It is not a portfolio or case studies site (that may come later).
- It is not a blog or content hub.
- It does not need to rank for broad keywords — SEO is not the acquisition strategy.
- It should not feel like a corporate agency site or a SaaS product page.
- It does not need to serve multiple audiences — it has one audience, and it should speak directly to them.

---

## Languages

The site should support English and German. English is the primary version and should be built first. German is required before launch — the business is based in Germany and a German-language presence is both professionally expected and legally relevant (imprint and privacy policy are legally required in Germany).

---

## Legal Requirements

As a German-based business, the site must include:

- **Imprint** (Impressum) — legally required in Germany
- **Privacy Policy** (Datenschutzerklärung) — legally required under GDPR

Both must be present in English and German before the site can go live.

---

## Integrations

- **Calendly** — for booking discovery calls directly from the contact page
- **Formspree** (or equivalent) — for the contact form, no server required
- No CMS, no database, no complex infrastructure — this is a static site
